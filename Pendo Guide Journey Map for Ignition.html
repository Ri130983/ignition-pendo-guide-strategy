Hard caps that prevent fatigue: Max 1 proactive guide per session · 24h minimum gap between any two guides · Max 3 GTC/X invites in 45 days · Drift-back nudges capped at 2 per 30 days · Global "Messaging Pause" segment for any account a CSM flags.

Week 5 May 29 – Jun 4 Cool-off — no new tranches, observe data Week 2: tooltip · Week 3: banner · Week 4: modal · continued per-user cadence
How we keep email "first" without delaying Pendo: Both channels launch May 1. At the user level, the Pendo modal is held back 48 hours after that user's tranche email sends, and suppressed entirely if they click the email within 7 days. That way customers who engage via email never get an in-app duplicate, and customers who ignore email still see Pendo on their next login.

Hard dependencies for May 1:
(1) Tina's finalized Wave 1 list is uploaded to Pendo by Jenna as an account-code list with tranche assignments (1–4). Segments reference the uploaded list, so re-uploads flow through automatically.
(2) Marketing automation publishes tranche_email_sent_at and tranche_email_clicked_at per user into Pendo metadata. Without those two fields syncing, the suppression rules can't fire.

The eight rules every guide follows

Rule 1
One job per surface
Invite, orient, celebrate, or ask — pick one.
Rule 2
Exit + memory
Always dismissible, dismissal always remembered.
Rule 3
Resurface on a schedule
10–14 day intervals — never on impulse.
Rule 4
Cap the noise
Max 1 proactive guide per session, globally.
Rule 5
Earn the feedback ask
3+ sessions or 14 days, whichever comes first.
Rule 6
One message, two channels
Email + Pendo say the same thing in the same week.
Rule 7
Design for the skeptic
Calibrate copy and pacing to the busy, the protective.
Rule 8
Instrument first
No guide ships before its events and segments are verified.
